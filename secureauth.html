üí° Security Best Practices

Essential tips to keep your accounts secure

‚úì Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Never reuse passwords across different accounts. Each account should have a unique, strong password with at least 12 characters combining uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.

‚úì Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of security by enabling 2FA on all accounts that support it. Use authenticator apps like Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator instead of SMS when possible.

‚úì Beware of Phishing Attacks

Always verify the sender's email address. Never click suspicious links or download attachments from unknown sources. Universities and banks will never ask for your password via email.

‚úì Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and apps. Security patches fix vulnerabilities that hackers could exploit.

‚úì Use a Password Manager

Password managers help you generate and store strong, unique passwords securely. Consider using tools like Bitwarden, 1Password, or LastPass for multiple devices.

‚úì Avoid Public WiFi for Sensitive Tasks

Public WiFi networks are often unsecured. Avoid accessing banking or entering passwords on public networks. If necessary, use a VPN for encryption.

‚úì Check Account Activity Regularly

Review your account login history and activity regularly. Most services show recent login locations and devices. Report any suspicious activity immediately.

‚úì Backup Important Data

Regularly backup your important files to multiple locations (cloud storage and external drives). This protects you from ransomware and hardware failures.
